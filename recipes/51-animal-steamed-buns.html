Animal Steamed Buns

by YungBaoBei

Animal shaped buns for Lunar New Year

Having some fun with making a few fluffy animal buns! One of my favorite lunar traditions. Check out my video if you wanna learn techniques of animal shaping!
Full video-> https://youtu.be/pblMDRJroDI

Recipe:
Plain Buns

  • All Purpose Flour (800 grams)
  • Yeast (8 grams)
  • Sugar (40 grams)
  • Water (400 grams)
  • Canola Oil (as needed)

Alternative Buns

  • Pumpkin, Sweet Potato, etc (250 grams)
  • All Purpose Flour (300 grams)
  • Sugar (40 grams)
  • Yeast (3-4 grams)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Canola Oil (as needed)

Other ingredients for decoration: Food coloring, Dried Dates, Beans, etc

  1. Prepare your choice of starch (potato or pumpkin etc) and peel, cut into small pieces. Steam until it is extremely soft. This can take from 20-40 minutes depending on what you use. Potato takes much longer than Pumpkin.
  2. Mush and mix into a paste, then add the sugar and yeast, give it a mix. Start adding flour in increments and begin to knead the dough together. Dough should feel very flexible, but not sticky. Knead for about 5 minutes then let the dough rise for 30 minutes. Knead for another 3 minutes and let rest for 1.5-3 hours or until doubled in size.
  3. At this point if the dough still feels kinda sticky you can knead in some more flour. Knead into a ball and cover with saran wrap. Take small pieces of dough out and mold the dough into whatever shape you need. Watch video for some technique and examples.
  4. Once finished, let each bun rise for another 20 minutes and then steam for 15 minutes. Be sure you have some parchment paper under each buns. Done!

To prepare plain white dough:

  1. Mix a cup with water, yeast and sugar. Then add in increments into a bowl with flour and begin kneading. Same exact steps as above. Knead, rest, knead and rest. Then shape as needed.
