Wontons! (plus spicy version)

Recipe (10 servings: 50-100 wontons)

  • Ginger (3 slices)
  • Scallion (1 large)
  • Chinese Celery (5 small stalks)
  • Yellow Chives (large bunch)
  • Shrimp (1 pound)
  • Ground Pork Fat (1/4 pound)
  • Salt, White Pepper, Chicken Stock (1/2tsp)
  • Sesame oil (a touch)
  • Egg (1)
  • Corn starch (1 tsp)
  • Wonton Wrappers (50-100)
  1. Prep the ingredients: finely mince the ginger and scallions, roughly mince the celery and rough dice the yellow chives. Clean the shrimp by cutting the top half and washing in a sink, then smash them and roughly dice.
  2. Throw shrimp into a mixing bowl and add one egg white along with the ginger/scallions. Using a claw shape, mix this until sticky. Add the pork fat and continue to mix until paste-like and sticky. Pick up the mixture and throw it into the bowl, this will tenderize the meat very well.
  3. Add in the chives/celery and also some sesame oil and corn starch. At this point mix very gently to prevent damaging any of the vegetables, transfer to a smaller bowl for wrapping.
  4. Wrap the wontons using a small butter knife, and egg yolk as a glue.(I wish I could post short videos on here so I could show you the actual technique on how to wrap them up. But I have a very good guide on my youtube video, so check it out if you need visual representation :)
  5. You can steam or boil the wontons for 6-9 minutes. Steaming will give you a more tender consistency but is optional.... and DONE!

Sichuan Spicy Sauce (2 servings, use more as needed)

  • Garlic (2 cloves)
  • Ginger (3 slices)
  • Scallions (1 large)
  • Bao Bei Chili Oil (4 tbl)
  • Soy paste (1.5 tbl)
  • Sugar (1/2 tsp)
  1. Mince everything very well and add in sauces.. mix. Drizzle as much as you want on top of freshly cooked wontons and garnish with scallions.
